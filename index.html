Abstract

As mobile robots are widely deployed in complex environments, reliable obstacle avoidance has become a critical challenge in motion planning. Traditional global planners falter in dynamic scenarios, while reactive local methods lack global guidance and easily fall into local minima. Recent diffusion-based planners demonstrate strong multimodal trajectory generation but suffer from high inference latency and poor generalization across robot constraints.

We propose HiFlow, a novel hybrid planning framework that integrates global A* guidance with an efficient constraint-aware generative local planner — FlowPlanner. Built on conditional Consistency Flow Matching, FlowPlanner generates entire safe and kinematically feasible avoidance trajectories in a single inference step, satisfying explicit safety (distance) and dynamic (curvature) constraints while preserving multimodality. Extensive experiments in static, dynamic, mixed-obstacle, and unseen zigzag scenarios show that HiFlow significantly outperforms A*+DWA, Diffusion Policy, LDP, and other SOTAs in success rate, SPL, and computation time.
